Sri Lanka is home to a vibrant population of turtles, with five different species found along its shores. These species include the Olive Ridley turtle, the Green turtle, the Leatherback turtle, the Loggerhead turtle, and the Hawksbill turtle. The beaches of Sri Lanka are among the most important nesting sites for turtles in the Indian Ocean, with thousands of turtles coming ashore every year to lay their eggs in the sand.

Things you probrbly didnt know:

  1. Turtles have been around for more than 200 million years, making them one of the oldest reptile groups in the world.
  2. Turtles can retract their head and limbs into their shell for protection
  3. Sea turtles can hold their breath for several hours, and some species can even sleep underwater.
  4. The gender of some turtle species is determined by the temperature of the nest during incubation, with warmer temperatures producing more females and cooler temperatures producing more males
  5. Some turtles have a symbiotic relationship with fish, where the fish clean the turtle's shell and skin of algae and parasites.
  6. The leatherback sea turtle is the largest turtle species in the world, and can weigh up to 2,000 pounds (907 kg).
