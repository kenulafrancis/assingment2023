There Are Five Main Turtle categories In Sri Lanka

Green Turtles
(Chelonia mydas)

Green turtles are the most commonly encountered sea turtle species in Sri Lanka. They have a streamlined body and can grow quite large. Green turtles are herbivorous, feeding primarily on seagrass and algae. They are known for their migratory behavior, often returning to the same beaches where they were born to lay their eggs. Green turtles play a vital role in maintaining the health of seagrass beds and are an important part of the marine ecosystem.

Loggerhead Turtles
(Caretta caretta)

Loggerhead turtles are larger in size compared to green turtles. They have a distinctive large head and strong jaws. Loggerheads are omnivorous and have a varied diet, including crabs, mollusks, and jellyfish. They are known for their powerful swimming ability and can cover long distances during their migrations. Loggerhead turtles are considered an endangered species globally,and their conservation is crucial to maintaining the balance of marine ecosystems.

Hawksbill Turtles
(Eretmochelys imbricata)

Hawksbill turtles are named after their pointed beak, resembling that of a bird. They have a unique and beautiful shell with intricate patterns. Hawksbills primarily feed on sponges but also consume other invertebrates. Due to the stunning appearance of their shells, they were historically hunted for the tortoiseshell trade, although this practice is now illegal. Hawksbill turtles are critically endangered globally, and conservation efforts focus on protecting their nesting sites and raising awareness about their importance.

Olive Ridley Turtles
(Lepidochelys olivacea)

Olive Ridley turtles are the smallest and most abundant sea turtle species worldwide. They have a high-domed shell and a wide distribution, including Sri Lanka. Olive Ridleys exhibit an incredible nesting behavior called arribadas, where hundreds or even thousands of females gather to nest simultaneously. They are omnivorous, feeding on crustaceans, jellyfish, and small fish. Like other sea turtles, Olive Ridleys face threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and accidental capture in fishing gear, emphasizing the need for conservation efforts.

Leatherback Turtles
(Dermochelys coriacea)

Leatherback turtles are the largest of all sea turtle species and are unique because they lack a hard shell. Instead, their carapace is covered with a thick, leathery skin. They have a more hydrodynamic body shape compared to other turtles, allowing them to dive to incredible depths. Leatherbacks have a diet consisting mainly of jellyfish. These turtles face various threats, including entanglement in fishing gear and ingestion of marine debris.Their conservation is crucial to protect their population and the balance of the marine ecosystem.

 The lifespan of green turtles is estimated to be around 80 years, although some individuals can live longer

Loggerhead Turtles
(Caretta caretta)

Loggerhead turtles are known for their large heads and powerful jaws, which enable them to feed on hard-shelled prey such as crustaceans and mollusks.

They have a wide distribution and can be found in various oceanic regions

Loggerhead turtles are listed as a threatened species due to various threats, including habitat loss and accidental capture in fishing gear.

 The lifespan of loggerhead turtles is estimated to be around 50 to 67 years, although some individuals have been known to live longer

Hawksbill Turtles
(Eretmochelys imbricata)

Hawksbill turtles are known for their distinctive beak-like mouths and beautiful shell patterns.

They primarily feed on sponges but also consume other invertebrates and seagrasses.

Hawksbill turtles are critically endangered due to factors such as habitat loss, pollution, and illegal trade of their shells.

 The lifespan of hawksbill turtles is estimated to be around 30 to 50 years, although some individuals may live longer

Olive Ridley Turtles
(Lepidochelys olivacea)

Olive Ridley turtles are known for their unique mass nesting behavior, called "arribadas," where thousands of females come ashore to lay their eggs simultaneously.

They primarily feed on jellyfish, shrimp, and other small invertebrates.

Olive Ridley turtles have a wide distribution and can be found in tropical and subtropical waters.
