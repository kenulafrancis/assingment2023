benthota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka is an important conservation project dedicated to the protection and preservation of turtles. Located in the town of Bentota, along the western coastline of Sri Lanka, the hatchery plays a crucial role in safeguarding turtle eggs and rehabilitating injured turtles.

Things Related with Bentota Turtle Hatchery:

  • One of the primary objectives of the hatchery is to protect turtle eggs from natural predators and human interference. Left on their own, turtle eggs face risks from predators and can be vulnerable to poaching. The hatchery provides a safe environment where the eggs can be incubated until they hatch, ensuring a higher chance of survival for the hatchlings
  • In addition to protecting eggs, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery also focuses on the rescue and rehabilitation of injured turtles. Turtles that have been injured at sea due to factors such as fishing nets or boat motors are brought to the hatchery for treatment and care. Once the turtles have been rehabilitated, they are released back into their natural habitat, promoting their chances of surviva
  • The hatchery serves as an educational center, raising awareness about the importance of turtle conservation and the need to protect these magnificent creatures. Visitors to the Bentota Turtle Hatchery have the opportunity to learn about the different turtle species found in Sri Lanka's waters, including the impressive leatherback turtle and the beautiful hawksbill turtle
